Recent role

I've spent my professional life (7 years) in different roles focusing on WEB software at dynamic, fast-growing and ever-changing tech startups. In my recent role as POS expert (4.5 years) at Treatwell's product team, it was my mission to lay the ground for gaining and retaining legal compliance of a Point of Sale software module in 10 different western European markets .

In essence, I researched the vast variety of legal requirements in a market, broke them down and owned meeting all non-technical ones while at the same time conveying technical requirements to the dev team and closely consulting them on delivery.
Becoming the go-to-guy for all newly emerged POS related topics and queries and occasionally stepping in as Product Manager, I acquired special knowledge about fiscalisation across Europe, slowly realizing that I highly enjoy the collaborated work and its constant challenges and I'd rather acquire generic web development skills to continue working in this field without being bound to a single - let's face it - rather uninspiring topic.

Career change

Consequently, and since I'm a quick learner, have proven to be a problem solver, good at mathematics and logics, excel at hours of focused, autonomous & challenging work paying great attention to detail and can tell from experience, that software development is where I want to work, I had decided to aim for a career change:
First go on a sabbatical year and then move from product to tech and acquire what I'm lacking - coding skills - to not only work with web developers but become one myself.




Project

This website is my final project of the very first technical module as part of the Web Development Course at Digital Career Institute (DCI). During this module ('User Interface Basic') I've learned how to design & build websites with HTML & CSS first and then expanded on the basics of Sass and Bootstrap. Now, it's time to showcase what I've learned up to this point.


Given the scope and purpose of this project and its limitations regarding some of my ideas, I've decided against using Bootstrap and instead build everything from scratch without using any framework or template.
But since I'm looking forward to collaborating with others and value structured and DRY coding, I've made use of Saas.
If you'd like to see my code, please refer to the related GitHub repository. If you want to learn more about what other skills I'm about to acquire during the course at DCI, please give this article a read. Next up will be JavaScript & React.
